VAE for Alzheimer’s Data Augmentation

A Variational Autoencoder (VAE) model designed to generate synthetic MRI data for Alzheimer’s disease, enhancing dataset size and improving diagnostic model performance.

Understanding the Problem

Alzheimer’s disease research is limited by small, imbalanced MRI datasets, which hinder the training of robust diagnostic models. Manual data collection is costly and time-consuming.

Our VAE solution generates high-quality synthetic MRI images, augmenting datasets to improve model accuracy and support early Alzheimer’s diagnosis.

How VAE for Alzheimer’s Works

A streamlined process using variational autoencoders to generate synthetic MRI data, enabling robust training of Alzheimer’s diagnostic models.

Input MRI Data

The VAE takes real MRI scans from Alzheimer’s datasets, preprocessed to standardize dimensions and normalize intensities for consistent input.

Latent Space Encoding

The encoder compresses MRI data into a latent space, capturing key features of Alzheimer’s-related brain patterns using a probabilistic distribution.

Synthetic Data Generation

The decoder samples from the latent space to generate realistic synthetic MRI images, preserving pathological features for Alzheimer’s diagnosis.

Model Training Enhancement

Synthetic data augments training datasets, improving the performance of downstream diagnostic models with up to 15% higher accuracy in validation tests.

Innovation & Learnings

Developing the VAE for Alzheimer’s data augmentation challenged us to balance generative model fidelity with medical accuracy. Fine-tuning the latent space to capture Alzheimer’s-specific brain features required precise optimization.

The project deepened our expertise in generative AI, MRI preprocessing, and data pipeline design, enabling scalable augmentation for medical imaging applications.

Most importantly, we learned the impact of synthetic data in overcoming dataset limitations, boosting diagnostic model performance and supporting early Alzheimer’s detection.
